Modding my Ukulele

This page is under construction

Back in 2014, I went through that ukulele phase that plagued many a millenial. It sat firmly within the youtube/ flower crown corners of the internet, succeeding the famous moustache craze of 2012. As a former violinist, playing a four stringed instrument like the ukulele was refreshingly easy. Everything was simplified into frets and chords. No extra strings. No sightreading. All was good and I played up until some point in the pandemic, when unfortunately, working in a medical company during lockdown threw many of my hobbies off-kilter. I recently found my ukulele again, so I dusted it off and made the decision to ease myself back into music. I found myself getting frustrated that the open strings span less than an octave. I couldn't pick out a melody and it felt like it was lacking depth. I had (what I thought was) a genius idea to swap out the G for a low G. I did a bunch of googling, reading up on nylon versus wound strings, guitar strings and all the options. I listened to videos of people playing with different strings and tunings. When I couldn't find what I was looking for, I took myself into Powell's in Galway, fully expecting to have to buy a guitar string. To my amusement, the shop had a bunch of options, and my unique(TM) idea was actually pretty standard. And, as you've probably guessed by now, the title here is defintely an exaggeration. I'm literally just changing the strings. (image of strings) I settled on buying a set of Aquila Nylgut.
